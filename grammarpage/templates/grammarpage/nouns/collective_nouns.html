{% translate "Collective Nouns" %}

{% translate "A collective noun is a word that refers to a group of people, animals, or things as a single unit. Examples include family, team, and flock. Although they refer to multiple members, they are typically treated as a singular noun." %}

{% translate "Verb Agreement Rules" %}

{% translate "Whether to use a singular or plural verb with a collective noun depends on the context and the dialect (British vs. American English)." %}

  • {% translate "Singular Verb (as a single unit):" %}

    {% translate "Use a singular verb when the group is acting together as one unit." %}

    • The team is celebrating its victory.
      {% translate "The entire team is acting as one entity." %}
    • The committee has decided on a new policy.
      {% translate "The committee is making a single, unified decision." %}
  • {% translate "Plural Verb (as individual members):" %}

    {% translate "Use a plural verb when the members of the group are acting individually." %}

    • The team are trying on their new uniforms.
      {% translate "Each member of the team is trying on their own uniform." %}
    • The committee are arguing about the proposal.
      {% translate "The members of the committee are in disagreement." %}

{% translate "BrE vs AmE Usage" %}

  • {% translate "American English (AmE):" %}

    {% translate "Typically favors singular verb agreement for collective nouns, treating the group as a single unit in most cases." %}

    The band is playing tonight.
  • {% translate "British English (BrE):" %}

    {% translate "More often uses plural verb agreement, especially when referring to groups of people, emphasizing the individual members." %}

    The government are not listening to the people.
