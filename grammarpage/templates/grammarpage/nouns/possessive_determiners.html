{% translate "Possessive Determiners" %}

{% translate "A possessive determiner is a word that shows who or what owns something. They are also known as possessive adjectives because they always come before a noun and act like an adjective." %}

{% translate "Unlike possessive pronouns (mine, yours, hers), possessive determiners are always followed by a noun." %}

{% translate "The Possessive Determiners" %}

{% translate "Here is a list of the possessive determiners and the personal pronouns they correspond to:" %}

{% translate "Personal Pronoun" %} {% translate "Possessive Determiner" %} {% translate "Example" %}
ImyThis is my book.
youyourWhat is your name?
hehisHe lost his keys.
sheherHer parents are very kind.
ititsThe cat is washing its tail.
weourThis is our house.
theytheirThey sold their old car.

{% translate "Key Characteristics" %}

  • Always precede a noun:

    {% translate "A possessive determiner cannot stand alone. It must be followed by a noun." %}

    This is my car.
  • Do not use with articles:

    {% translate "You do not use an article (a, an, the) with a possessive determiner." %}

    the my car -> my car
  • Distinction between 'its' and 'it's':

    {% translate "Its is the possessive determiner (e.g., The dog wagged its tail). It's is a contraction of it is or it has." %}
