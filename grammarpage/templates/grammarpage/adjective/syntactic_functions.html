{% translate "Adjective: Syntactic Functions" %}

{% translate "In a sentence, adjectives primarily perform one of two syntactic functions: they can either describe a noun directly within a noun phrase (attributive function) or serve as the complement of a verb (predicative function)." %}

{% translate "1. Attributive Function" %}

{% translate "An adjective used attributively modifies a noun or pronoun directly. It typically comes right before the noun it describes." %}

  • This is a beautiful flower.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}beautiful{% translate "' is part of the noun phrase '" %}a beautiful flower{% translate "' and describes 'flower'." %}
  • He saw an old house.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}old{% translate "' modifies the noun 'house'." %}

{% translate "Note: In some cases, usually with multi-word adjectives or for emphasis, the adjective can come after the noun (e.g., " %}'a man brave and true'{% translate ")." %}

{% translate "2. Predicative Function" %}

{% translate "An adjective used predicatively describes the subject of a sentence, but it is separated from the subject by a verb, typically a linking verb like " %}'to be', 'to seem', 'to become'{% translate ", etc." %}

  • The flower is beautiful.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}beautiful{% translate "' follows the verb 'is' and describes the subject 'flower'." %}
  • The soup tastes delicious.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}delicious{% translate "' follows the linking verb 'tastes' and describes the subject 'soup'." %}
