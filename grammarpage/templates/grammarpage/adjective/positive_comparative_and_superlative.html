{% translate "Positive, Comparative, and Superlative Degrees" %}

{% translate "Adjectives are words that describe the qualities of nouns. They can exist in three different forms, or 'degrees,' to show a comparison between things." %}

{% translate "1. The Positive Degree" %}

{% translate "The positive degree is the basic, unmodified form of the adjective. It simply describes a quality without making any comparison." %}

  • This is a tall building.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}tall{% translate "' describes the building's height without comparing it to anything else." %}
  • She has a beautiful voice.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}beautiful{% translate "' describes the voice's quality." %}

{% translate "2. The Comparative Degree" %}

{% translate "The comparative degree is used to compare two things. It shows that one thing has a certain quality to a greater or lesser extent than another." %}

  • This building is taller than that one.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}taller{% translate "' compares the height of two buildings." %}
  • My voice is more beautiful than hers.
    {% translate "The phrase '" %}more beautiful{% translate "' compares the quality of two voices." %}

{% translate "3. The Superlative Degree" %}

{% translate "The superlative degree is used to compare three or more things. It indicates that one thing has the highest or lowest degree of a certain quality among a group." %}

  • This is the tallest building in the city.
    {% translate "The adjective '" %}tallest{% translate "' indicates that this building surpasses all others in height." %}
  • She has the most beautiful voice of all.
    {% translate "The phrase '" %}most beautiful{% translate "' indicates that her voice is the best among a group." %}
