{% translate "The Zero Article: With Proper Nouns" %}

{% translate "Proper nouns, which are unique names of people, places, and organizations, generally do not take an article. This is because they are already specific by their very nature." %}

{% translate "1. Names of People and Titles" %}

{% translate "Do not use an article with personal names or titles, unless a specific rule applies (e.g., family names)." %}

{% translate "Correct" %} {% translate "Incorrect" %}
Mary is my best friend. The Mary is my best friend.
President Biden gave a speech. The President Biden gave a speech.

{% translate "Exception: Use the with a plural family name to refer to the whole family: 'The Smiths are coming for dinner.'" %}

{% translate "2. Most Countries, Cities, and Continents" %}

{% translate "As a general rule, we do not use an article with the names of most countries, cities, or continents." %}

  • {% translate "Countries:" %} Germany, Japan, Egypt.
  • {% translate "Cities:" %} Paris, Rome, Sydney.
  • {% translate "Continents:" %} Africa, Europe, Asia.

{% translate "Exceptions: Remember to use the with countries that have a plural name or include a descriptive word like 'republic' or 'states': the United States, the Netherlands." %}

{% translate "3. Single Mountains, Islands, and Lakes" %}

{% translate "Unlike mountain ranges or groups of islands, individual geographic features typically do not take an article." %}

{% translate "Zero Article (Single)" %} {% translate "With 'The' (Group/Range)" %}
Mount Everest is the highest peak. The Himalayas are a major mountain range.
We visited Lake Geneva. We visited the Great Lakes.
