{% translate "Tense Sequence in Indirect Speech" %}

{% translate "These examples demonstrate how verb tenses systematically change when converting direct speech to indirect speech, particularly when the reporting verb is in the past tense." %}

{% translate "Comprehensive Tense Sequence Examples" %}

{% translate "The following examples show complete tense transformations with explanations:" %}

{% translate "Present Simple → Past Simple" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I work at a bank," he said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} He said he worked at a bank.

{% translate "Note: The present simple tense shifts to past simple in reported speech." %}

{% translate "Present Continuous → Past Continuous" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I am studying English," she said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} She said she was studying English.

{% translate "Present Perfect → Past Perfect" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I have finished my homework," they said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} They said they had finished their homework.

{% translate "Past Simple → Past Perfect" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I saw that movie," he said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} He said he had seen that movie.

{% translate "Future Simple → Conditional" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I will call you," she promised.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} She promised she would call me.

{% translate "Modal Verb Transformations" %}

{% translate "Modal verbs undergo specific changes in indirect speech:" %}

{% translate "Direct Speech" %} {% translate "Indirect Speech" %} {% translate "Example" %}
can could "I can swim" → She said she could swim
may might "I may go" → He said he might go
must had to "I must leave" → She said she had to leave
shall should "I shall return" → He said he should return

{% translate "Mixed Tense Examples" %}

{% translate "Real-life examples often contain multiple tense changes:" %}

{% translate "Complex Statement" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I have been working here since I graduated, and I will continue until I find a better job," he said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} He said he had been working there since he had graduated, and he would continue until he found a better job.

{% translate "Question with Multiple Tenses" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "Why haven't you finished the work I gave you yesterday?" she asked.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} She asked why I hadn't finished the work she had given me the day before.

{% translate "Special Cases and Exceptions" %}

{% translate "Some situations don't follow the standard backshift rules:" %}

{% translate "General Truths" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "The sun rises in the east," the teacher said.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} The teacher said the sun rises in the east.

{% translate "Note: General truths remain in present tense." %}

{% translate "Reporting Immediately" %}

{% translate "Direct:" %} "I am hungry," he says.

{% translate "Indirect:" %} He says he is hungry.

{% translate "Note: No backshift when reporting verb is in present tense." %}
